Navigating apps using Voice command without click or swipe gestures

Project Name

Navigating apps using Voice command

Project Summary

Using Voice to navigate apps is a tedious task. Imagine instead using swipe and click gestures you use voice commands to access your photos or buying a cloth at online shopping site. First the app should understand your language you speak. And you can't open a specific photo in your photos app just by saying "Open the photo where I am standing" "Open the photo with Umbrella". Its impossible to open a specific photo by explaining the object or theme present in the photo. What if the photo has Numbers specified on it. It's easy to open the photo by saying "Open photo Number 5". Same with shopping site, if each product has a Product ID. Its easy to say, "Open product 121", "Add product 121 to cart" "Delete product 121 from cart", instead of saying "Open Blue berry shirt slim fit with size L". This demo shows how we can navigate apps using Voice Gestures/Commands and implementation with Visual Aid to perform a task within the app.

The disadvantage is, mobile is personal, and user who do not want to reveal any information do not use voice over to chat or send messages while traveling in a public transport. Except in few cases, voice commands is used only in private situations where user is at home or when he is alone. And all apps should be integrated with a single Voice Assistant to navigate through app and its very difficult to just complete the task only with voice over.

Problems found during User research

  1. Currently Voice assistant use command/gestures which is limited for searching or opening apps or play music.
  2. Navigating an app like selecting/adding/editing/deleting a photo, browsing through the store of shopping site and checkout is not implemented in apps
  3. Voice commands are more often used while in private situations or when the user is alone.
  4. Voice over cannot be used in public transport or in a crowded place which is a threat to privacy.
  5. There are many voice assistants like, "Siri", "Alexa" and "Google". Voice commands need to be trained individually for the voice assistants. Integrating to apps to perform a complete task(add a product to cart and checkout without click or tap)becomes tedious job.
  6. In some cases use of visual aid like text informing the product id, the number sequence in photos, to remove specific notification from mobile is necessary for the users to make their life easy to perform the task.
  7. Voice commands should work for apps with different context like, banking, shopping, news, billing, calendar, notes, chat and others.
  8. Voice assistants need to understand the dialect of each user and different languages present in the world.
  9. Touching and feeling an app(by click or tap) along with haptic feedback is more convenient for the user. Visual or Sight is the first sense and visual information stores easily in our mind. Hearing or auditory comes second. Imagine how many voice commands should a user need to remember for each and every app. Why not combine Voice commands with visual aid while navigating the app.
  10. Natural Language Processing or NLP should be trained for each and specific task.

Learning from the project

With advent of voice enabled devices without visual information some commands like doing a banking transaction may put user at security risk.

We cannot rely only on Voice command to perform all the tasks within a app. Visual Aid along with voice commands helps the user to perform the task efficiently

Voice assistant should understand any specific command from user, natural language used in any dialect.

Voice assistants with different dialect should be trained and it needs to understand the app context.
